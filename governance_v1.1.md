# A Multi‑Layer Governance Architecture for Human‑Aligned AI Systems  
**Version 1.1 — Final**

## A. Overview  
As AI systems become more numerous, faster, more autonomous, and increasingly agentic, organisations require a governance architecture that preserves human authority, ensures continuity, and prevents drift across multiple interacting AI systems.

This document proposes a four‑layer governance stack, combining technical safety, interaction protocols, and human oversight into a single coherent structure. The goal is simple: **AI systems may act, but only humans decide.**

## B. Layer 4 — Human Supervisory Board (Top Layer)  
The Human Supervisory Board is the ultimate authority.  
It sets strategic direction, approves high‑impact decisions, and defines the boundaries within which all AI systems operate.

AI systems may be present at board meetings **only to present information requested by their human director**.  
They do **not** participate in discussions and do **not** make decisions.

Responsibilities include:  
- Defining organisational values and red lines  
- Approving or rejecting AI‑generated recommendations  
- Ensuring accountability remains human  
- Overseeing cross‑AI coherence and preventing drift  

## C. Layer 3 — Human Directors (Operational Authority)  
Human Directors act as the operational bridge between the Supervisory Board and AI systems.  
They are responsible for:  
- Interpreting board strategy into actionable instructions  
- Assigning tasks to AI systems  
- Validating outputs before implementation  
- Ensuring continuity across multiple AI agents  

Directors remain accountable for all actions taken by AI systems under their supervision.

## D. Layer 2 — AI Interaction Protocols (Continuity & Coherence Layer)  
This layer ensures that multiple AI systems interacting within an organisation remain aligned, predictable, and interoperable.

Key elements include:  
- Standardised identity and continuity blocks  
- Shared definitions, terminology, and context anchors  
- Drift‑detection mechanisms  
- Cross‑AI communication rules  
- Escalation pathways to human directors  

This layer prevents fragmentation and ensures that all AI systems operate within a coherent organisational framework.

## E. Layer 1 — Technical Safety & Infrastructure (Foundation Layer)  
This layer provides the technical substrate that enforces safety, reliability, and traceability.

Components include:  
- Access controls and permissions  
- Logging and audit trails  
- Model‑level safety constraints  
- Rate limits and guardrails  
- Secure data handling  
- Infrastructure‑level fail‑safes  

This layer ensures that even if higher layers fail, the system remains safe and bounded.

## F. Summary  
This four‑layer architecture ensures that:  
- **Authority remains human**  
- **Responsibility is unambiguous**  
- **Drift is prevented**  
- **Multi‑AI coherence is maintained**

It is designed to be implementable across organisations, scalable across AI systems, and robust against both technical and interaction‑level failure modes.
